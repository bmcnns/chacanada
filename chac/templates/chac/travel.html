Travel Information

The event is scheduled to take place at Dalhousie Hall, Dalhousie University and the one-day international conference is being planned to tie-in with Resolution 68/237 of the United Nations (UN) that officially declared the decade of 2015-2024 as the International Decade for Peoples of African Descent: Recognition, Justice and Development. Therefore, in acknowledging and highlighting this decade, Creole Heritage Association Canada (CHAC), and Creole Heritage Association International (CHAI), and its affiliated bodies are recognizing that people of African Descent represent a distinct group whose human rights must be promoted, protected and preserved.
